## 1. What Cheezus Is

Cheezus is a cheese discovery, logging, and education app for anyone who takes food seriously. Users log the cheeses they eat — with ratings, tasting notes, and photos — building a personal cheese diary that grows smarter the more they use it. The app pairs that personal history with a structured knowledge base (Cheezopedia), a hierarchical cheese database spanning 1,200+ cheese types and producer-specific versions, smart pairing recommendations, AI-powered label scanning, and a map-based discovery layer that surfaces nearby cheese shops, producers, and events.

It is not a recipe app. It is not a restaurant guide. It is the definitive tool for anyone who wants to track, understand, and deepen their relationship with cheese — whether they are logging a supermarket Cheddar or reviewing a Super Gold from the World Cheese Awards.

The analogy is Vivino for wine. Vivino turned a casual wine drinker into a connoisseur by giving them a scan, a rating, and a community. Cheezus does the same for cheese — a category with equal complexity, equal cultural depth, and zero dominant digital tool.

## 2. The Problem

Cheese is one of the most complex, culturally rich food categories in the world. More variety, more terroir, more craft than almost anything else people buy and eat regularly. Yet there is no dedicated digital home for cheese lovers that matches what Vivino has done for wine or Untappd for beer.

The current tools are inadequate. Cheese.com is a static web database with no personal logging, no community, and no mobile experience. iGourmet is an ecommerce site. Generic food apps treat cheese as a macro, not a subject. When someone tries an extraordinary Comté at a wine bar and wants to remember it, rate it, and understand what to drink with it, there is nowhere to go.

Beyond personal logging, there is an education gap. Most cheese lovers know the names — Brie, Cheddar, Gruyère — but have no context for the differences between producer versions, origin regions, milk types, or ageing periods. Cheezus turns every tasting into a learning moment. A user who logs a Manchego starts to understand the difference between 6-month and 12-month aged versions, what milk type makes it distinctive, and why it pairs so naturally with Rioja.

The social gap is equally real. Cheese is inherently social — cheese boards, tasting evenings, farmers' market visits — but there is no shared space where enthusiasts can follow other tasters, see what friends are trying, or benchmark their palate against a community. The tools people reach for (Instagram, notes apps, memory) are inadequate substitutes for a purpose-built product.

## 3. Target Market

### Primary audience
Food-literate adults aged 25–45. Home entertainers who buy cheese with intention. People who already spend money on quality cheese and want a tool that adds lasting value to that spend. Wine drinkers who pair naturally. People who have stood at a deli counter or a farmers' market stall feeling curious but slightly overwhelmed.

This is a high-willingness-to-pay segment. They already spend money on good food. A premium subscription to understand and remember what they are eating is a trivial financial decision relative to what they spend at the cheese counter.

### Market size
The global specialty cheese market is valued at over $28 billion and growing at approximately 4% annually. The UK alone has over 700 named cheese varieties and a well-established artisan cheese culture. Vivino has 65+ million users; Untappd has 11+ million. Cheese sits between these two categories in cultural prestige and consumer interest, with no dominant app.

A specific lens: if 1% of the actively cheese-curious adult population in the UK downloads Cheezus in year one (approximately 200,000 downloads), at a 10% paid conversion rate, that is 20,000 Pro subscribers.

### Secondary audiences

- **Sommeliers and food professionals:** Authoritative users who add credibility to the community. Their presence elevates the perceived quality of the platform.
- **Cheese producers and affineurs:** The producer showcase feature gives artisan producers a rich, mobile-first presence. World Cheese Awards integration makes Cheezus the natural companion app for the awards circuit.
- **Brands and specialty food companies:** The B2B sponsored content model creates a non-intrusive advertising channel for honey producers, wine merchants, cracker brands, and charcuterie makers wanting to reach a food-literate audience at the exact moment of cheese selection.
- **Food educators and WSET students:** The Cheezopedia provides structured cheese knowledge with genuine educational depth.
- **Gift buyers:** A Cheezus Premium subscription as a gift for the cheese lover in someone's life — easy to communicate value, obvious audience.

### Geographic focus
UK first. Deep artisan cheese culture, a dense network of independent cheesemongers, strong food media, and the World Cheese Awards is a UK institution. France and Italy second — the cheese-producing nations where enthusiasm is highest. US and Australia third — large English-speaking markets with growing specialty food cultures.

## 4. Competitive Landscape

### Direct competitors
There are no direct competitors with a polished, mobile-first product dedicated to cheese discovery and personal logging at this quality level. The space is genuinely underserved in a way that mirrors where wine apps were in 2010.

### Adjacent products

**Vivino:** The explicit model Cheezus aspires to replicate for cheese. Vivino's core loop — scan a label, get a rating, log it, get recommendations — is structurally identical to what Cheezus does for cheese. Built to 65+ million users and a $825 million valuation from a single mechanic applied consistently to a passionate niche.

**Untappd:** Beer's equivalent — 11+ million users built around check-ins, ratings, and a badge system. Demonstrates that highly engaged niche communities around a food and drink category sustain significant product businesses. Cheezus's gamification design is directly informed by Untappd.

**Cheese.com:** The largest cheese database online. Web-only, no logging, no ratings, no community, no mobile experience. A static reference, not a product. Cheezus takes the same data ambition and builds a living product around it.

**MyFitnessPal:** Owns food logging but treats cheese as a nutritional entry, not a culinary experience. A user logging Manchego in MyFitnessPal sees macros. A user logging Manchego in Cheezus sees origin, milk type, pairing suggestions, tasting notes, and community ratings. Different product entirely.

### Competitive advantage
Cheezus has a combination no competitor offers at consumer pricing:

1. **Hierarchical cheese database** — Cheese Types (Brie) → Producer Cheeses (Président Brie). Users log specific versions, contributing to aggregate type ratings. No other app structures this correctly — this is the data foundation that makes discovery and recommendation meaningful.
2. **AI label scanner** — GPT-4o Vision reads a cheese label and auto-fills the logging form. The friction between "I want to remember this" and "logged and rated" is reduced to under 30 seconds.
3. **Cheezopedia** — A comprehensive knowledge base with articles, recipes, guides, and pairings. Bi-directional linking between content and cheese pages makes every article a discovery surface.
4. **Producer showcase pages** — Cinematic, CMS-driven pages with video heroes, team stories, gallery sections, and awards showcases. No other consumer cheese app gives producers a presence of this quality.
5. **Location-based discovery** — Mapbox integration surfaces nearby cheese shops, producers, and events with GPS-accurate distance.
6. **Sponsored content model** — B2B revenue that keeps the consumer experience free while creating real value for brands. Sponsored pairings and articles are native to the experience, not banner ads, and always provide generic alternatives to maintain user trust.
7. **World Cheese Awards integration** — QR codes at the world's most prestigious cheese competition link each cheese to its Cheezus entry and producer showcase. No competitor can replicate this.

**B2B Sponsored Pairings:**

- **Premium (£10/month):** Everything in Basic plus homepage feed placement with "Featured Partner" treatment.

**B2B Sponsored Articles:**
- **Standard (£8/month):** Branded article in Cheezopedia — recipe, guide, or educational content.
- **Featured (£15/month):** Article plus homepage feed promotion.

### Why this model works

The target user is precisely who artisan food brands want to reach: food-literate, quality-conscious, and actively spending on premium food. A wildflower honey brand paying £10/month to appear as the recommended pairing for a soft-rind cheese is not advertising to a disinterested audience — it is a service to a user who genuinely wants that recommendation.

The "alternatives always provided" policy is the critical trust mechanism. A sponsored pairing always shows generic alternatives alongside the branded product. This keeps the user relationship primary and the commercial relationship secondary — the only model that sustains trust in a niche community.

The product is built. The focus is getting it into the hands of the right people first — people who will use it deeply, rate it highly, and recommend it authentically.

- **Food media:** Decanter, The Guardian Food, Delicious Magazine, Borough Market blog. "The app that knows more about cheese than the person behind the counter" writes itself.
- **Cheesemonger partnerships:** Independent cheesemongers as brand ambassadors. A card at the counter: "Log this cheese in Cheezus." Zero cost, high trust, perfect context.
- **Personal channels (RandomShenans, realtalk.ing):** Authentic documentary content about building and using the app. Real cheese, real tastings, real discoveries.

Food content creators are the most efficient distribution channel for a niche app with genuine product quality.

- **Cheese TikTok and Instagram:** A thriving community of cheese content creators — from cheesemongers to home enthusiasts. Seeding the app to 20–30 creators who already make cheese content generates authentic review and discovery content.
- **"The Vivino of cheese" content:** A search-optimised format for editorial content, social posts, and App Store metadata that captures anyone already familiar with wine apps.
- **Recipe and food blog integrations:** Cheezopedia content is shareable and linkable. Every recipe that references a Cheezus article page is a download opportunity.

### Phase 3: World Cheese Awards push (Autumn 2026)
The World Cheese Awards 2026 is the product's marquee annual moment.

- **Producer onboarding:** Reaching the top 100 producers at the Awards to populate their showcase pages before the event. A producer page with video, team story, and award history is a compelling value proposition.
- **On-site QR marketing:** Physical materials driving scans → downloads → first cheese logged. The highest-intent user acquisition possible.
- **Press and PR:** A solo developer building the definitive cheese app, tied to the world's most prestigious cheese awards, is a compelling narrative for food journalism.

### Phase 4: International expansion and partner portal (2027)
- French and Italian localisation (the two most cheese-literate producing nations)
- Partner portal launched as a self-serve web product (spec complete, build pending)
- B2B channel growth with artisan food brands beyond specialist cheese companies

### Viral mechanics
The product has two layers of built-in virality:

1. **The cheese box share:** A user's personal cheese diary — 87 cheeses across 14 countries, 4.2 average rating — is a shareable stat. It goes in bios, in stories, and in conversations. Every shared box is a silent referral.
2. **The pairing recommendation:** When a user discovers a spectacular wine-cheese pairing through the app and shares it, that recommendation carries the app name. Every shared pairing is an ad with social proof from someone the recipient trusts.

## 7. Technical Architecture

### Stack
- **Frontend:** React Native + Expo (SDK 54), TypeScript, Expo Router (file-based navigation)
- **Backend:** Supabase (PostgreSQL, Auth, Realtime, Storage)
- **AI Label Scanning:** OpenAI GPT-4o Vision API
- **Maps:** Mapbox (`@rnmapbox/maps`) — location discovery with nearby shops, producers, events
- **State:** React Context API
- **Video:** `expo-video` (producer showcase video heroes)
- **Image Handling:** expo-image-picker + expo-sharing
- **Typography:** Inter (body) and SpaceGrotesk (headings)
- **Build/Deploy:** EAS Build + EAS Submit (iOS App Store + Google Play)

### Why this stack

Expo enables iOS and Android from a single codebase with a solo developer. Supabase provides auth, database, storage, and real-time subscriptions in one platform at costs that scale appropriately. The PostgreSQL foundation enables the complex queries the recommendation engine requires — taste profile analysis, fuzzy search scoring, proximity-based discovery — without a custom backend.

GPT-4o Vision as the label scanner is the critical onboarding decision. The scan-to-log flow reduces the friction of adding a new cheese from several minutes of manual entry to under 30 seconds. This is the moment where new users become retained users.

The hierarchical database design — Cheese Types → Producer Cheeses — is the foundational architectural decision that separates Cheezus from anything else on the market. When a user logs a Président Brie, they contribute to the aggregate rating for all Brie (the type) and for Président Brie specifically (the producer version). Over time, Cheezus builds the most authoritative, community-validated cheese database in existence — a moat that compounds with every user and every log.

The personalised feed algorithm runs entirely in PostgreSQL RPC functions, executing taste profile analysis server-side. This makes the algorithm lightweight on the client, fast to load, and keeps user preference data within Supabase.

### Scalability

The architecture has no meaningful scaling constraints at consumer scale:
- Supabase handles concurrent read traffic with connection pooling and read replicas
- The AI label scanner is a stateless API call per scan — scales horizontally with OpenAI
- Mapbox handles map rendering client-side with CDN tile delivery
- The largest single operation — personalised feed generation for a user with hundreds of entries — resolves in a few hundred milliseconds server-side

The current database holds 1,198 cheese types and 1,192 producer cheeses, fully indexed for search and recommendation. Scaling to 10,000 cheeses is a data entry task, not a technical one.

One pre-production security item: the OpenAI API key needs to move from the client environment variable to a Supabase Edge Function before significant user volume. Currently adequate for the current stage but documented as a concrete next step.

## 8. Content Strategy

### The Cheezopedia IS the content

Every Cheezopedia entry — cheese variety profile, recipe, pairing guide, producer story — is a piece of content with standalone SEO value and in-app utility. The content strategy is not to create content about Cheezus. It is to create content about cheese that brings users into Cheezus.

A recipe for "The Perfect Brie en Croûte" should rank on Google. A producer profile for a Neal's Yard Dairy cheese should appear when someone searches that name. The bi-directional linking between articles and cheese pages means every piece of content deepens the discovery layer and every cheese detail page surfaces relevant content.

### Content pillars

**"What do I do with this cheese?":** The most common question at a cheese counter and the most underserved one digitally. Cheezopedia answers it with recipes, pairing guides, and serving suggestions linked directly to the cheese in the user's box. This is the most actionable content type and the strongest reason to return to the app between purchases.

**Producer stories:** Behind every great cheese is a person, a place, a process. The cinematic producer showcase pages — video hero, team portraits, gallery, award history — are editorial content that serves both the producer's marketing needs and the user's curiosity. A tasting that starts with "I wonder who makes this" ends with a producer documentary inside Cheezus.

**Sponsored editorial:** Partner brands contributing genuinely useful content (recipes, pairing experiments, serving guides) under their brand within Cheezopedia. The content must stand alone as valuable to earn placement. A honey brand writing "The science of sweet-salty pairing: why honey with aged cheese works" is food education that also drives conversion. Both things can be true.

**World Cheese Awards coverage:** The awards are the app's annual content calendar anchor. Pre-awards (cheeses to watch), during awards (live coverage), post-awards (winner profiles, where to buy) creates three high-intent content moments from exactly the target audience, each driving downloads and engagement.

### RTH Content System integration
- Cross-promotion with Scavvi (RTH's other app) — different audience, same founder, occasional crossover for content involving food and outdoor activity
- Cheezus as the recurring food content anchor in Ross's channels, reactivated each season (Christmas boards, summer hosting, World Cheese Awards)

## 13. Why This Works

The insight at the centre of Cheezus is simple: cheese deserves the same quality of digital companion that wine has had for fifteen years.

Vivino built an $825 million business by making wine approachable, personal, and community-validated. Every core mechanic — scan a label, get a rating, log it, get recommendations — maps directly to the cheese use case. The database structure is obvious. The hunger exists. The tools (GPT-4o Vision, Supabase, Expo) now allow a solo developer to build what would have required a team of twenty in 2010.

The hierarchical cheese architecture is the structural decision that matters most. A user who logs a Président Brie contributes to the aggregate rating for all Brie. A user who logs a Montgomery's Cheddar contributes to the aggregate for all Cheddar. Over time, Cheezus builds the most authoritative, community-validated cheese database in existence — a moat that compounds with every user and every single log entry.

The World Cheese Awards connection is the distribution cheat code. The most cheese-knowledgeable, cheese-enthusiastic people on the planet are concentrated in one venue twice a year. Cheezus has QR codes in that room. That is a level of qualified acquisition that no paid marketing budget can replicate.

The B2B revenue model is the structural advantage. Because artisan food brands want to reach exactly the same audience Cheezus is building, the sponsored content layer generates meaningful revenue without compromising the user experience. The app stays free for consumers; brands pay to be useful rather than to interrupt.

The product has two modes of success. In the first, it becomes the Vivino of cheese — the default tool for food-literate consumers who take their cheese seriously, with a loyal niche community and reliable subscription revenue. In the second, the B2B sponsored content layer scales to become the primary revenue driver as the audience grows, with artisan food and drink brands paying meaningful sums to reach the most food-literate audience in any app category. Both paths start with the same product. The cheeses are already in the database. The question now is who finds the app first.
